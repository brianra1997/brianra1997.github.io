Design Philosophy

My name is Brian Ra and I am an Interaction Designer from the metro Atlanta area. For those unfamiliar with Interaction Design, it is designing the way users interact with products. In simple terms, my goal is to make a product as easy to use as possible. My design philosophy starts with truly understnading the users' goals, wants, and needs to create a design that will stand out amongst my competition. Putting the user first is my number one priority. This is achieved through thorough research, interviews, communication, and most importantly, empathy.

Here are some of my works

During my time at Kennesaw State University, I collaborated with my peers to create projects using design processes such as Goal Directed Design (GDD) and Lean Ux. These processes include user interface design, prototyping, sitemapping, storytelling, modeling, usability testing, and mock up creation.

Projects

Student Abroad

An Interaction Design project implementing the Goal Directed Design Process. The goal of this project was to streamline the study abroad process. Through user research and interviews, usability testing, and iterating upon our findings, our group worked to create a pleasant user experience for a process that can be quite frustrating.

TatWho

For this project, my team, consisting of collegues from KSU's "Interaction Design II" course, were tasked with creating a mobile application while implementing the Lean UX design process. We decided to create a mobile app that would help users find tattoo artists that fit their needs. Using agile methods, our team worked in two week "sprints" or fast paced, iterative design periods to create a minimal viable product as quickly as possible.

MUSE

For my "Senior Project" class, my team was tasked to create a mobile application of our choosing, while implementing the Lean UX design process. Our app, MUSE, was designed to help users overcome "writer's block". We conducted multiple interviews throughout the process, implementing ideas from each to iterate upon the app while looking back on our sprint backlogs to make sure our priority was always the user.

PUSH

For my "Visual Design" course, we were tasked to create a mobile application with the goal of teaching users a task or activity. My application, PUSH, was designed with the goal of teaching users the basics of skateboarding. This includes terms and skills that would not be known to the average person without prior knowledge of skateboard culture.
